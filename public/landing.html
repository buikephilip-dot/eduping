The Problem

Nigerian schools are running
on manual processes

Every day, communication gaps slow down administrators, frustrate parents and overwhelm teachers. The tools available were built for different markets.

01
Parents are out of the loop
Parents only hear about homework, attendance or performance issues when it's too late. Phone calls are missed, notes get lost, and broadcast lists are impersonal and one-sided.
02
Attendance is unreliable
Paper registers are slow, easy to falsify and impossible to analyse. Admin has no real-time view of which students or teachers are present on any given day.
03
Fee collection is chaotic
Tracking who has paid and who owes is entirely manual. Money slips through the cracks and records are inconsistent across terms and classes.
04
No data-driven decisions
Schools cannot identify at-risk students early, track teacher performance objectively, or generate meaningful reports without spending hours collating data.
